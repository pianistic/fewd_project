Marine Science Instructor

Description:

Our Marine Science Instructors teach groups of 4th - 12th grade students our marine science, oceanography and island ecology curriculum. Instructors are responsible for implementing an active, hands-on, experientially-oriented program including snorkeling, kayaking, marine labs, hiking, and a variety of evening programs. This is a high energy program where instructors spend long days working and teaching in a unique environment.

Requires a BA or BS degree in Marine Biology, Biology, Oceanography or related science. Must possess excellent water skills, Current certification in Lifeguard Training, First Aid, and CPR required at the start of the contracted period. (Training may be conducted on site.) Teaching experience with youth is desired. Flexibility and ability to work with others as a team and live in a remote residential setting is essential. Room and board are provided on-site.

Food Services

Description:

At CIMI and Astrocamp, Food is Fun! We provide 3 meals a day for up to 300 people. We are always looking for good, strong team players with background and experience in all aspects of the food service industry, including salad preparation, breakfast, lunch, and dinner shifts, baking, menu preparation, and large group preparation.

Our clients range in age from 8 years to adult, and we serve buffets, family-style and salad bars. We offer a wide variety of nutritious and delicious meals and accommodate special diet and vegetarian needs.

Summer SCUBA Dive Staff

Description:

Catalina Sea Camp is the largest diving camp for young people in the world, issuing over 400 certifications to teens each summer. We seek NAUI instructors and divemasters to teach SCUBA classes of all levels to young people at camp. We teach a wide variety of diving classes including Skin Diver, Passport Diver, Scuba Diver, Advanced, Underwater Ecology, Research, Underwater Photo, Underwater Video and Master Diver. Aside from fabulous diving, Catalina Sea Camp provides on-site room and board and salary. Employment is from early June to mid August.

An excellent opportunity for personal growth exists at CIMI. You can teach/assist classes you've never taught before, work with equipment (video or photo) that you may not have had the opportunity to use before, and dive in beautiful places. The quality of staff we have had is remarkable and our expectations are very high. At Sea Camp the hours can be long and, at times difficult, but the chance to positively impact and influence a large number of children always exists. If these are things that excite and interest you, we encourage you to apply.ve in a remote residential setting is essential. Room and board are provided on-site.

Sea Camp Sail Staff

Description:

One of our most popular programs at Catalina Sea Camp is our Sail Program. We seek instructors that have a sailing background in all types of boats, primarily dinghy sailing. Some of our instructors are certified by the American Sailing Association (ASA) and US Sailing. Our classes range from Basic Sailing in Lasers, to Intermediate Sailing in Vanguard 420s, to Coastal Cruising aboard our 22ft keelboat. We even offer Board Sailing/Windsurfing classes too. Aside from amazing wind and sailing fun, Catalina Sea Camp provides on-site room and board, plus salary. Employment runs from early June to mid August.

An excellent opportunity for personal growth exists at Catalina Sea Camp. You can teach/assist classes you've never taught before, and work with boats that you may not have had the opportunity to sail before. We also train you how to operate different types of safety powerboats, as well as certify you as a lifeguard and in CPR (if not already certified).
